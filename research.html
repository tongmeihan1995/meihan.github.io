Published & Forthcoming Papers


with Bin Xu
North American Chapter of the Association for Computational Linguistics, 2020, Accepted.

Event extraction aims to identify an event and then extract the arguments participating in the event. Despite the great success in sentence- level event extraction, events are more natu- rally presented in the form of documents, with event arguments scattered in multiple sentences. However, a major barrier to promote document- level event extraction has been the lack of large-scale and practical training and evalua- tion datasets. In this paper, we present Do- cEE, a new document-level event extraction dataset including 27,000+ events, 180,000+ ar- guments. We highlight three features: large- scale manual annotations, fine-grained argu- ment types and application-oriented settings. Experiments show that there is still a big gap between state-of-the-art models and human beings (41% Vs 85% in F1 score), indicat- ing that DocEE is an open issue.
